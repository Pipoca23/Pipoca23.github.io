Empadinha

These little savory pies are a traditional Brazilian party food but also a popular afternoon snack sold at the corner smoothie shops. They look like tiny two-crust pies. The most common fillings are chicken, hearts of palm or shrimp.

The secret to a good empadinha is to have a moist filling and just the right amount of dough. You don't want them too doughy because they'll be tough. It is a labor-intensive recipe because you must prepare the dough, roll it out and line your little tins first. Make the filling and let it cool before spooning it into the dough-lined tins. Finally, you have to roll out small pieces of dough into circles that you will use to cover the tins before putting htem in the oven to bake.

In this recipe, the empadinhas are filled with shrimp.

INGREDIENTS

For the dough:

  • 400 g all-purpose flour
  • 200 g butter at room temperature
  • 1 egg
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Preparing the dough:

Sieve the flour and salt onto your working surface to form a mound and make a well in it.

Blend the butter with the eggs using your fingertips and add to the well, kneading slowly from the middle towards the edges, without overworking the dough.

Cover with plastic film and let the dough rest in the fridge while you make the filling.
